# Location Reference Guide

## Denmark

### Copenhagen

#### Østerbro
- **Ole's Apartment:** Main residence, where suitcase is examined
- **Østerbrogade:** Location of jeweler (diamond appraisal) and locksmith

#### Heimdalsgade
- **Garage:** Rented storage unit where Porsche is kept

#### Other Copenhagen Locations
- **Central Station:** Meeting point for Ole and Malene
- **Café Django:** Where Ole and Malene first discuss the treasure
- **Metropolitanskolen:** Ole's school (elite high school)
- **Shooting Range:** Outside Copenhagen, private club (Malene's father's membership)

### Odense (Funen)

#### Ole's Childhood Home
- **Address:** Unspecified residential street near city center
- **Features:**
  - Guest room under the eaves
  - Crawl space behind bookcase (where suitcase hidden)
  - Built by Ole's grandfather
- **Significance:** Where suitcase was hidden for 67 years

#### Other Odense Locations
- **Local Church:** Site of father's funeral
- **Jewelry Store:** Where Lillian worked (mentioned in Ch. 27)

### Aarup (Funen)
- **Aarup Auto Salvage:** Where Ole's father stored the Porsche
  - Owner: Arne Brix
  - Features: Climate-controlled warehouse
  - Porsche stored for 2 years

### Silkeborg (Jutland)
- **Summer House:** Where Ole and Malene open the chest
  - Deep in forest, invisible from road
  - Half-kilometer rutted track access
  - Small lake nearby
  - No neighbors

## Italy

### Lake Como Region

#### Dongo
- **Type:** Small lakeside town, western shore of Lake Como
- **Historical Significance:** Where Mussolini was captured (April 27, 1945)
- **Key Location:** Piazza Barachini (main square)
  - Restaurant where theft occurred (1945)
  - Town hall (white and yellow, 4 stories)
  - War memorial (stone pedestal, bronze eagle)

#### Albergo Dongo
- **Type:** Hotel
- **Manager:** Alberto (on organization's payroll)
- **Features:** Ole and Malene's base for 14 nights
- **Staff:** Includes "the waiter" (Marco) - organization operative

#### Garzeno
- **Type:** Small mountain village above Lake Como
- **Access:** Winding road from shore
- **Features:**
  - Village square
  - Shuttered church
  - Largely abandoned/depopulated

#### The Stone House
- **Location:** Hilltop ~1km east of Garzeno square
- **Features:**
  - Visible from town square
  - Stone walls, tile roof
  - Wooden shutters
  - Abandoned since WWII
  - Shed with tools (pick and shovel)
- **Significance:** Reference point for buried treasure

#### Treasure Burial Site
- **Location:** 50 meters due north of house's north corner
- **Marker:** Flat, almost circular stone (natural formation)
- **Depth:** Approximately 1 meter
- **Contents:** Iron chest with brass fittings, booby-trapped

### Milan
- **Marshaling Yard:** Where POWs were sorted (1945)
- **Outskirts:** Route for Ole and Malene's escape

### Rimini
- **POW Camp:** Where SS prisoners were sent for processing

## Architectural/Setting Notes

### The Crawl Space (Odense)
- Access: Small door in knee wall
- Hidden behind: Bookcase holding Salmonsen's Encyclopedia (26 volumes)
- Contents: Cold, dark, dusty, smells of old wood
- Size: Large enough to hide suitcase deep inside

### The Stone House (Italy)
- Construction: Stone walls, tile roof
- Condition (1945): Recently abandoned
- Condition (2019): Derelict, shutters hanging
- Interior: Kitchen, living room (fireplace), bedroom
- Outbuilding: Shed with tools

### The Chest
- Material: Black iron, brass fittings, steel-lined walls
- Weight: ~30kg (25kg chest + contents)
- Lock: Two keyholes, requires both keys turned simultaneously
- Booby trap: Explosives triggered by forced entry
- Contents: ~8kg of gemstones (mostly diamonds)
