## Dual Timeline Structure

The novel alternates between two timelines:

### Present Day (2019)
- Chapters: 1, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9, 11, 12, 14, 15, 17, 18, 20, 21, 23, 24, 25, 26, 28, 29
- Focus: Ole and Malene's treasure hunt
- Antagonist chapters interspersed

### Historical (1945, 1952)
- Chapters: 2, 4, 7, 10, 13, 16, 19, 22, 27
- Focus: Jens's journey from theft to death
- Provides context and emotional weight

### Pattern
Generally alternates: Present → Past → Present → Past
With some variations for pacing (consecutive present-day chapters in Act Three)

## Chapter-by-Chapter Summary

- **POV:** Helga Jantzen
- **Setting:** Koblenz, 2019
- **Summary:** Elderly woman receives mysterious package containing diamonds and letter about her uncle Paul
- **Function:** Flash-forward, establishes promise fulfilled

- **POV:** Ole
- **Setting:** Odense/Copenhagen, April 2019
- **Summary:** Ole's father dies; funeral; discovers hidden door and suitcase in crawl space

## Thematic Through-Lines

### The Promise
- Paul extracts promise from Jens (Ch. 4)
- Jens writes it in letter (Ch. 3, 27)
- Ole and Malene debate honoring it (Ch. 5, others)
- Promise fulfilled (Ch. 29)

### Guilt and Redemption
- Jens's war crimes haunt him
- Ole's estrangement from father
- Treasure as both curse and opportunity

### The Weight of History
- 75 years between theft and recovery
- Three generations affected
- Past never truly buried
