# Character Reference Guide

## Main Characters

### Ole Henriksen
**Role:** Protagonist (present day)

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Age | 42 (in 2019) |
| Born | ~1977 |
| Occupation | High school teacher (mathematics/physics) at Metropolitanskolen, Copenhagen |
| Residence | Østerbro, Copenhagen |
| Family | Father (deceased April 2019), mother (deceased 1991, cancer) |
| Relationship to Jens | Grand-nephew (Jens was his grandmother Lillian's brother) |

**Personality:**
- Introverted, routine-oriented, risk-averse (initially)
- Estranged from his father for years
- Transforms from passive observer to decisive actor
- Capable of violence when protecting loved ones

**Character Arc:**
- Bored teacher → treasure hunter → killer (in self-defense) → wealthy but changed man
- Key transformation: Chapter 28 (kills two men to save Malene)

**Physical Description:**
- Not explicitly detailed; implied average build, needs fitness training for the expedition

### Jens Raben
**Role:** Historical protagonist, Ole's great-uncle

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Born | ~1922 (age 23 in 1945) |
| Died | 1952 (age ~30), heart surgery complications in Kiel |
| Nationality | Danish |
| Military Service | SS, 16th SS Panzergrenadier Division Reichsführer-SS |
| Family | Sister: Lillian Raben (later Henriksen) |

**Timeline:**
- 1941 (age 19): Enlisted in SS
- 1941-1944: Eastern Front (witnessed/participated in atrocities)
- 1945: Italy, meets Paul Fleischer
- April 27, 1945: Steals Mussolini's diamonds in Dongo
- April 1945: Buries chest, Paul dies in ambush
- May 1945: Captured by Americans, handed to Danes
- 1945-1949: Prison camp (4 years)
- 1949: Returns to Odense, works in factory
- 1949-1952: Lives in Germany under name "Dieter Schmidt"
- September 1952: Hides suitcase in sister's crawl space
- September 1952: Dies during heart surgery in Kiel

**Personality:**
- Idealistic youth corrupted by war
- Emotionally numb after Eastern Front experiences
- Loyal to promises (honors commitment to Paul despite everything)
- Survivor mentality

**Key Possessions Left Behind:**
- Suitcase containing: SS uniform, Walther P38 pistol, Soldbuch, letter, two brass keys, diamonds (proof stones)

### Paul Fleischer
**Role:** Jens's comrade, dies in 1945

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Rank | Hauptsturmführer (SS Captain) |
| Nationality | German (Bavarian) |
| Age at Death | ~late 20s-early 30s (estimated) |
| Died | April 1945, partisan ambush above Garzeno |
| Family | Niece: Helga Jantzen (née Fleischer), Koblenz |

**Role in Story:**
- Mastermind of the diamond theft
- Driver of Mussolini's limousine
- Makes Jens promise to share treasure with his family
- His dying wish drives the moral arc of the entire novel

**Last Words:** "Promise me you'll find my family. My niece Helga. She lives in Koblenz, Florinspfaffengasse."

## Antagonists

**Role:** Leader of neo-fascist organization's Dongo operation

| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Age | 70s |
| Base | Rome (palazzo on Via dei Coronari) |
| Role | Coordinates surveillance of Dongo area and Fleischer family |
| Died | Chapter 28 (accidentally shot by his own man) |

- Patient, calculating, cultured

## Historical Figures (Mentioned)

### Benito Mussolini
- Appears briefly in Chapter 2
- Fleeing north toward Switzerland
- Diamonds stolen from him in Dongo
- Executed by partisans April 28, 1945

### Clara Petacci
- Mussolini's mistress
- Present during the theft
- Her overheard comment ("One was German, the other Danish") is how the organization knows to watch for Danes
